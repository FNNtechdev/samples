Mental Health: Why Talking About It Matters

By Dr Faith Njoroge • July 13, 2025

Mental health is just as important as physical health, yet for many years, it has been ignored, misunderstood, or even stigmatized. In today's fast-paced world, more people than ever are experiencing anxiety, depression, and emotional burnout — and often, they suffer in silence.

Why It’s Okay to Talk

Talking about mental health helps break the stigma. When we open up, we not only help ourselves heal, but also give others the courage to speak out. Conversations create safe spaces, and in those spaces, healing begins.

How to Start the Conversation

You don’t need to be a professional therapist to make a difference. A simple "How are you really doing?" can go a long way. Listening without judgment, offering support, and just being present are powerful steps.

Seeking Help is Strength, Not Weakness

There’s no shame in seeking therapy or taking medication. Just as you would see a doctor for a broken bone, your mind deserves the same level of care and attention.

Final Thoughts

Let’s make it normal to talk about mental health — not as a taboo, but as a vital part of our well-being. You never know whose life you might change just by starting the conversation.
